Visual Hierarchy

Simply Chocolate

simplychocolate.dk

Simply Chocolate, shown in the image above, is a great example of using size and scale to emphasize visual hierarchy. The first thing that attracts the design is the huge text that explains what the product is about. This is done in a way that doesn't detract from the other elements on the screen.

White Space and Clean Design

McBook Air - Apple

apple.com

In this website, Apple execute active whitespace throughout the website well by placing imagery strategically to draw the users eye to specific elements down the page, but the company also uses passive whitespace to guide the user through the content without a hitch.

Repetition

Obermayer

obermayer.com

On this website we can see the repetition in many elements such as: Navigation, which, being always gray with white text, helps to clearly differentiate it from the content of the page. Logo, the logo has a circular graphic with an arrow, depending on the space different versions of the logo are used; in addition to other elements that are repeated on the website.
